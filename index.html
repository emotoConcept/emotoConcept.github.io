emoto

Speculative work reimagining how we
relate to mobile and smart home devices

From phone,

Emoto is a robotic platform that gives body and expression to a concept AI operating system

We made it to better explore some of the following concepts + interactions -
  • Placemaking for Phones
    When you get home, everything else has its place (and time): the coat on the hanger, shoes on the rack, and keys by the door. But your phone? In your hand and on your mind, in bed, while you're working, and in the bathroom? We refer to placemaking as the process of finding or making a place of belonging for an idea, device, or service in someone's life. That includes consideration of people's behaviors and habits, not just physical where and how. Being intentional about placemaking, is being intentional about how you frame interaction with said idea/device/or service.
  • You might not have somewhere specific place for your phone, but you'll always need to charge! The robot is a wireless charging base.
  • The touchscreen GUI and functionalities of the phone become accessible far-field through the AI character
Extended Sensor systems
  • Computer vision for contextual awareness. This particular example is of object tracking.
  • Face Recognition allows for tailored behavior and varied levels of access as it becomes shared device for the space
  • The phone takes on the role of home-assistant as it responds to speech and sound input
Augmenting Phone Capabilities
  • Dismissing a notification because the robot knows you saw it, or learning some tact about whe it's appropriate.
  • When not being actively engaged, the robot defaults to being your timekeeper
  • Motors and smart cv enabling a level of telepresence. Example shown is a facetime
Device as character
  • Nonverbal communication with gestures, eye gaze, and body language. It attempts to understand and see what has caught your attention when you actively engage.
  • Learning some contextual awareness (not shown is the conversationally generated 'shortcut' of the concept OS)
  • Tailored behavior to each person as they relate to the phone. There are varied levels of access as it becomes a shared device for the space
